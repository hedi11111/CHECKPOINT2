Space Exploration

Space exploration is the ongoing discovery and exploration of celestial structures in outer space by means of continuously evolving and growing space technology. While the study of space is carried out mainly by astronomers with telescopes, the physical exploration of space is conducted both by unmanned robotic space probes and human spaceflight.

Historical Background

The early era of space exploration began with the launch of artificial satellites in the late 1950s. The Soviet Union's Sputnik 1 was the first artificial satellite, followed by the United States' Explorer 1. This marked the beginning of a new era in space science.

Milestones in Space Exploration

Some significant milestones include:

  • Yuri Gagarin's orbit of the Earth in 1961.
  • The Apollo 11 moon landing in 1969.
  • The Mars rover landings, such as Curiosity in 2012.
